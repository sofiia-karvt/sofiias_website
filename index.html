Cotton Recycling

Old clothes currently make up about 6% of all household trash in Israel. Therefore, recycling the clothes can save up to 15 times the total energy required in the process of disposing of the clothes.

In order to produce 1 kg of cotton, approximately 8,000 liters of water are needed! Therefore, reusing clothes is more environmentally friendly and can even be a factor in huge water savings.

Ways to Recycle Cotton

Upcycle

Transform your clothes into useful items like tote bags, pillowcases, or even rugs.

Repurpose as Cleaning Rags

Turn old t-shirts or towels into effective and eco-friendly cleaning cloths.

Swap Clothes

Host a clothes swap with friends or sell gently used items online to give them a new home.

Use for Craft Projects

Cut up fabric scraps from clothes to make quilts, decorations, or art projects.
